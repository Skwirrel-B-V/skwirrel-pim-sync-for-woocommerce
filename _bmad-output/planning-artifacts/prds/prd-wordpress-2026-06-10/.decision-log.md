## Discovery decisions

1. **PRD is forward-looking, not retro-documentation.** The plugin works well today (v3.10.2); this PRD defines the *next chapter* — a quality/maturity theme, not a new-feature land-grab.
2. **Core problem named by user:** "the plugin as is is nice, but becomes complex … simple to use and more in control." Complexity is accumulating as the product grows.
3. **Sharpest pain = admin UX:** "all kinds of settings and relations … really hard for customers to use." Logging exists ("we log things") but "could be better integrated."
4. **The defining problem = fault isolation:** "people have issues, but is it our plugin or the whole stuff with other plugins." With WP 7.0 out, the "is it us or the environment?" question is the heart of the PRD.
5. **Audience = non-technical:** "store owners, marketeers, people that don't want to invest in a good webshop." Drives the simplicity imperative.
6. **Diagnostics goal = customer self-service:** "customer self service is always the goal."
7. **Success definition (user, verbatim):** "updates in wp wont harm this. complete control and fast syncs." → three success dimensions: (a) resilience to WP/WC/ecosystem updates, (b) customer sense of complete control, (c) fast syncs (performance is a first-class success measure).
8. **Horizon = WP 7.0 era.** Concrete timing anchor; pairs with the 7.0+ primary / 6.9+ floor compatibility posture already set in `project-context.md` and v3.10.2.

## Three pillars of the PRD (synthesized + confirmed in spirit)
- **Pillar A — Tame configuration complexity** (simpler, legible admin UX; sensible defaults; guided setup).
- **Pillar B — Integrated diagnostics & fault isolation** (self-service "is it us or the stack?" answers; health, conflict/compat detection, environment report, surfaced sync state).
- **Pillar C — WP 7.0-era resilience on a well-tested, safe-update, fast-sync backbone** (NFR backbone made first-class).
